Top Study Techniques

Use methods proven by cognitive science to retain more information in less time.

🔁

Spaced Repetition

Review material at increasing intervals. Leverages the forgetting curve to lock knowledge into long-term memory.

🧪

Active Recall

Test yourself instead of re-reading. Retrieving information strengthens neural pathways far better than passive review.

🍅

Pomodoro Technique

Study in 25-minute focused blocks followed by 5-minute breaks. Prevents burnout and maintains peak concentration.

🗺️

Mind Mapping

Connect ideas visually to understand relationships between concepts. Great for complex subjects and holistic learners.

✍️

The Feynman Technique

Explain any concept in simple terms as if teaching a child. Gaps in your explanation reveal what you still need to learn.

😴

Sleep & Memory

Your brain consolidates memories during sleep. Studying before bedtime and getting 7–8 hours maximizes retention.

🎯

Interleaving Practice

Mix different subjects or problem types in one session. Harder but builds more flexible and robust understanding.

📝

Elaborative Interrogation

Ask yourself "why" and "how" about the material. Deep processing leads to far superior memory formation.
